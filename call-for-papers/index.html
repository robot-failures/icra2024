Topics of interest

The topics of interest for the workshop include, but are not limited to, the following:
  • Techniques for robot execution monitoring, avoidance, and recovery
  • Approaches for preventing and handling failure propagation
  • Causal learning for failure understanding
  • Failures in HRI and HRC
  • Failures with humans in the loop
  • Role of failure explainability
  • Datasets and tools on how to study failures in HRI (such as augmented reality, virtual reality, extended reality)
  • Long-term studies on failures
  • Trust repair after failures have occurred
  • Role of LLMs in the ability to explain, correct, and alleviate errors
  • Human perception of robot failures
  • Role of human factors in HRI failures
  • Robot verification and/or testing approaches that consider failure scenarios

Submission guidelines

Submissions to the workshop can be up to 4 pages long using the IEEE conference template. Submissions describing work in progress that authors would like to discuss with the workshop participants are particularly encouraged and welcome. The workshop does not have official proceedings, but the submitted papers will be posted on the workshop website if the authors agree.

Paper submissions should be made via EasyChair. Please note that submissions via other means, such as email, will not be accepted.

Each submitted paper will receive at least two reviews, based on which an acceptance decision will be made.

Accepted papers will be presented in the form of spotlight presentations and during a poster session that will follow the spotlights.
